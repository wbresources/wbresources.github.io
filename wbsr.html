Workbench Student Rules

As Workbench is not a physical school, parental involvement is necessary for effective learning and to prevent students from becoming lazy. The following rules have been established to achieve these objectives, and they will be explained to the students by the administration.

Homeworks, Classworks, and Daily Planners General Submission Rules:

  • All submissions must be made via the MS Teams application chat feature as an attachment.
  • Classworks must be submitted to the respective subject teacher and the administration on the same day before 5 PM (UAE time).
  • Daily planner (planned) must be submitted to the administration one day prior at or before 9 PM (UAE time).
  • Daily planner (actual) must be submitted to the administration on the same day at or before 9 PM (UAE time).
  • Homeworks must be submitted to the respective subject teacher and the administration before the due time.
  • All submissions must be clearly scanned and converted into a .PDF file with the standard label.

Guidelines for Labeling Homework Files:

  • Type the label while posting the file.
  • Use two-digit dates for days and months except for the year (four digits).
  • There is a space after each fragment.
  • Choose the first three letters of each subject, except ICT, to denote the subject.
  • For Daily Planner, use DP.
  • Use CW for Classworks and HW for Homeworks.

Example:

Labels for Grade 9B

  • HW G9B SCI DUE DD-MM-2022
  • HW G9B ICT DUE DD-MM-2022

  • CW G9B MAT DUE DD-MM-2022
  • CW G9B SCI DUE DD-MM-2022
  • CW G9B ICT DUE DD-MM-2022

For the Daily planner (planned), the date should be the next day's.

  • DP(P) G9B FOR DD-MM-2022

For the Daily planner (actual), the current date should be used.

  • DP(A) G9B FOR DD-MM-2022

Classworks:

Students of grades 5 and above are required to take down their own notes along with the notes given by the respective subject teacher. Class notes are taken down in a notebook not computer typed. Students can always ask the teacher to wait if you need more time to note down the important points.

Homeworks:

Homeworks should be done neatly with sincerity. It’s important to understand that homeworks are for the students’ own benefit. Note: We don’t want the students to postpone their homeworks until the last moment of submission. Often students end up completing them in a hurry without any benefit. So, we want the students to do their works as soon as possible and if they have any doubt regarding the homework, they need to ask the teacher in the class itself. If the student is not able to submit on time. The subject teacher and the admin must be notified the reason. The admin will then verify this with the parent. Students who submit late or do not submit at all without any genuine reason will be made to redo the same work 5 or more times. Which is then send to the subject teacher and the admin. Marks also will be deducted from the continuous assessment of that term. Recurrent late submissions and no submissions will result in expulsion from the class. Homeworks done badly, hastily, with minimum or no effort at all will be considered as no submission.

Classroom and MS Teams rules:

Classworks:

  • Students in grades 5 and above are required to take their own notes along with the notes given by the respective subject teacher.
  • Class notes must be taken in a notebook, not computer-typed.
  • Students may ask the teacher to wait if they need more time to note down important points.

Homeworks:

  • Homeworks must be done neatly and with sincerity.
  • It is crucial to understand that homeworks are for the students' benefit, and they should not postpone their homeworks until the last moment of submission.
  • If students have any doubts regarding their homework, they need to ask the teacher in class.
  • If a student is unable to submit on time, the subject teacher and the administration must be notified of the reason.
  • Students who submit late or not at all without any genuine reason will be required to redo the work 5 or more times, which will be sent to the subject teacher and the administration.
  • Marks will also be deducted from the continuous assessment of that term. Recurrent late submissions and no submissions will result in expulsion from the class.
  • Homeworks done hastily, poorly, with minimal or no effort at all will be considered as no submission.

Classroom and MS Teams rules:

  • To ensure effective participation in the virtual class, students are required to have functioning IT equipment, including a microphone, headphones, camera, a speedy PC/laptop, and a strong internet connection.
  • They must also maintain a distraction-free environment at home.
  • The teacher will monitor each student's participation, and any student whose participation is deemed inadequate will receive a warning followed by expulsion from the class.
  • Students must join the class promptly when it begins, and those who arrive later than five minutes without a valid reason will not be allowed to attend that class.
  • Additionally, students must keep their cameras on during class, or they will be expelled.
  • Girls are required to wear respectable clothing with a hijab, while boys can wear thob or large-sized plain T-shirts/shirts. Hoodies, tight T-shirts/shirts are not permitted.
  • Students are allowed to use a blurred background only and are not allowed to use any virtual backgrounds.
  • They must not eat or drink during class, and they must attend from a designated study area in their homes.
  • Walking around or lying down during class is not allowed.
  • Students must come to class refreshed and prepared, and they must revise previously taught lessons before the class begins.
  • Teachers will ask questions during the first part of the class to ensure students have revised properly.
  • Any student found to have not revised properly will be punished accordingly.
  • Parents must report any leave to the admin.
  • Communication with teachers and classmates is only through MS Teams.
  • Students are not allowed to chat or hold conversations with their classmates using any other platform (WhatsApp, Zoom, etc.) unless they knew them before joining Workbench.
  • The chat feature is allowed for beneficial conversation only.
  • Any student found to be chatting during class or engaged in unproductive chat will be punished accordingly.
  • Recurrence of such incidents will lead to expulsion from the academy.
  • Students are not allowed to form any groups or channels in the MS Teams application without prior consent from the admin.
  • In case of any connection issue, students must contact the admin for assistance through MS Teams or WhatsApp.
  • All necessary materials for the class must be brought before the class begins.
